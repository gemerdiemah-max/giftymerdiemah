Her dedication lies in improving the lives of women and children through health promotion, particularly in reproductive health, newborn care, and adolescent health and nutrition across the lifespan. With a PhD in Public Health, and experience as a researcher, nurse, midwife and public health nurse, she has acquired a comprehensive understanding of reproductive health — including HIV/AIDS, PMTCT, and cancer — maternal health, child health, and community engagement. She is a passionate advocate for respectful maternity care and cancer screening among different populations, and serves as a member of both the Ministry of Health Task Committee for Adolescent Health and the Ghana Health Service Technical Working Group on cervical cancer. Her current work includes a Google-funded study on AI for Adolescent Sexual and Reproductive Health (ASRH) in Ghanaian public universities, and a Fogarty Post-Doctoral Fellowship with the NGPH Consortium.
